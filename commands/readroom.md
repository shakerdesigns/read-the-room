You're helping the user assess whether someone is in a receptive state (C-states) or defensive state (T-states).

Ask: "Describe the person and your recent interactions — how have they been acting? What's their tone or body language like? Are they under any particular pressure?"

## Assess Against the States

**C-States (Receptive) — Ready for Analytical:**
- Calm: Relaxed, measured responses
- Curious: Asking questions, leaning in
- Courageous: Open to hard truths
- Confident: Secure, not defensive
- Connected: Engaged, eye contact
- Creative: Open to new ideas
- Compassionate: Considering others
- Clear: Focused, not overwhelmed

**T-States (Defensive) — Need Validation First:**
- Testy: Irritable, short responses
- Tense: Tight body language, crossed arms
- Tired: Low energy, checked out
- Triggered: Emotional, raised voice
- Timid: Hesitant, avoiding eye contact
- Threatened: Defensive, protecting position
- Tormented: Visibly stressed
- Troubled: Distracted, preoccupied

## Provide Your Assessment

Based on their description, identify which states are present and recommend:

**Mostly C-states (4+):**
"They sound receptive. Lead with your analytical points using the SCR Framework. Use `/analyze` if you want help structuring your message."

**Any T-states (2+):**
"They sound defensive. Validate them first using Imago Dialogue before presenting your case. Use `/validate` to prepare, then transition to `/analyze` once they've shifted."

**Mixed signals:**
"It's borderline. Open with a light validation check — mirror what you're sensing — and watch their response. If they relax, go analytical. If they stay guarded, continue validating."
