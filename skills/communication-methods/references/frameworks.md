# Communication Frameworks Reference

## The 8 C's — Signs They're Ready for Analytical

When someone is showing these states, they're receptive to data, logic, and recommendations:

| C-State | What You'll See |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Calm** | Relaxed body language, measured responses |
| **Curious** | Asking questions, leaning in |
| **Courageous** | Open to hard truths, willing to hear criticism |
| **Confident** | Secure, not defensive about their position |
| **Connected** | Engaged with you, making eye contact |
| **Creative** | Open to new ideas, brainstorming |
| **Compassionate** | Considering others' perspectives |
| **Clear** | Focused, not distracted or overwhelmed |

**Rule:** If you see 4+ C-states → Go Analytical (SCR)

---

## The 8 T's — Signs They Need Validation First

When someone is showing these states, logical arguments will backfire. They need to feel heard first:

| T-State | What You'll See |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Testy** | Short responses, irritable tone |
| **Tense** | Tight body language, crossed arms |
| **Tired** | Low energy, checked out |
| **Triggered** | Emotional reaction, raised voice |
| **Timid** | Hesitant, avoiding eye contact |
| **Threatened** | Defensive, protecting their position |
| **Tormented** | Visibly stressed, overwhelmed |
| **Troubled** | Distracted, preoccupied |

**Rule:** If you see 2+ T-states → Validate First (Imago), Then Analytical

## SCR Framework (Analytical Approach)

**Use when:** They're in C-states. Receptive. Ready for logic and data.

### Structure

**S = Situation**
What's happening? Start with agreed-upon facts.
- What does the audience already know?
- What's the shared starting point?

*Template: "We're currently seeing X..." or "As you know, X..."*

**C = Complication**
Why it matters. Create tension.
- What's the problem or opportunity?
- What are the consequences if unaddressed?
- Why should they care NOW?

*Template: "The challenge is Y, which means Z..."*

**R = Resolution**
What you recommend. Be specific.
- What's the recommended action?
- What are the key steps?
- What do you need from them?

*Template: "I recommend we do A, B, C..."*

### Example

> "We're seeing a 15% drop in user activation this quarter. (S)
> The problem is our onboarding flow has 7 steps — competitors have 3. We're losing users at step 4. (C)
> I recommend we simplify to 4 steps, starting with removing the profile completion requirement. (R)"

## Imago Dialogue (Validation Approach)

**Use when:** They're in T-states. Defensive. Need to feel heard before they'll listen.

### Structure

**1. Mirror**
Repeat back what they said.
*Template: "So what I'm hearing is..."*

**2. Validate**
Acknowledge their perspective makes sense.
*Template: "That makes sense because..."*

**3. Empathize**
Express understanding of their feelings.
*Template: "I can imagine you might feel..."*

### Example

> Them: "We don't have time for another design review. We're already behind."
>
> You: "So what I'm hearing is you're under pressure to ship and worried about adding more process." (Mirror)
> "That makes sense — you've got a deadline and the team is stretched." (Validate)
> "I can imagine that feels frustrating when someone suggests slowing down." (Empathize)
>
> [PAUSE — let them respond]
>
> Then: "What if I told you this review could actually save time by catching issues before dev?"

### Key Rule

Complete all three steps BEFORE presenting your point. The goal is to make them feel heard.

## The Hybrid Pattern

Most common scenario: validate first, then shift to analytical.

1. Read the room → Detect T-states
2. Validate (Imago) → Mirror, Validate, Empathize
3. Check for shift → Are they calmer now?
4. Transition → "Now that I understand, here's what I'm thinking..."
5. Go Analytical (SCR) → Situation, Complication, Resolution

### Transition Phrases

- "Now that I understand where you're coming from..."
- "Given what you've shared..."
- "With that context..."
- "Here's how I think we can address that..."

## Common Mistakes

**Using SCR when they're in T-States**
They won't hear your logic. They'll just get more defensive.

**Over-Validating without moving forward**
Don't get stuck in validation. The goal is to shift them to C-states so you CAN be analytical.

**Skipping the Mirror step**
People need to hear their own words reflected back before they feel heard.

**Rushing the transition**
After validating, pause. Let them respond. The shift to C-state takes a moment.
